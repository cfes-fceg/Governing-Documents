\section{National Executive}
\label{national-executive}

\subsection{Overview of National Executive}
\label{overview-of-national-executive}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The National Executive is the elected leadership of the Federation and consists of the following five positions:

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    President
   \item
    Vice President Academic
   \item
    Vice President Finance and Administration
   \item
    Vice President External
   \item
    Vice President Services
   \item
    Vice President Communications

  \end{enumerate}

 \item
  The National Executive\textquotesingle s general tasks and responsibilities include:
  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Upholding the Constitution and Policy Manual of the Federation
   \item
    Organizing and directing the efforts of the Federation
   \item
    Ensuring the completion of all mandates
   \item
    Managing Commissioners within respective portfolios, and assuming responsibilities in the event they are not completed
   \item
    Ensuring communication is maintained to member schools by:

    \begin{enumerate}
     \item
      Ensuring all official communications are bilingual and gender neutral
     \item
      Ensuring updates on tasks done by each member of the CFES Team are compiled and distributed to members at least every two months
     \item
      Ensuring outcomes reports are completed for any meetings or other external events attended by a member of the CFES Team
    \end{enumerate}
   \item
    Making contact with government bodies when necessary
   \item
    Hiring working groups for their successors as required by April 30th
   \item
    Creating a summary transition document by April 30th to be stored in the electronic CFES archive
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{President}
\label{president}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The President's tasks and responsibilities include:
  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Ensuring the mandates of each Vice President are completed
   \item
    Coordinating the activities of the Board in conjunction with the Board Chair
   \item
    Facilitating the development of the Federation, and its members
   \item
    Ensuring that long range planning for the Federation is undertaken, with annual updates given to the membership
   \item
    Maintaining communication channels with all stakeholders of the Federation
   \item
    Ensuring that Action Plans are completed by each of the National Executive; these Action Plans are to be distributed to the Board prior to SDES.
   \item
    Preparing a transition document prior to CESS.
   \item
    Ensuring that each of the National Executive prepares a transition document prior to CESS.
   \item
    Promoting, collecting prizes and striking an awards committee for and presenting annual Awards each year at CELC.
   \item
    Ensuring communication with Engineers Canada and attending the Engineers Canada Annual General Meeting and Board Meetings, if feasible
   \item
    Attending the following conferences or finding a suitable representative:

    \begin{enumerate}
     \item
      BEST General Assembly
     \item
      BEST Summit on Development of Engineering Societies
    \end{enumerate}
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Vice President Academic (VPA)}
\label{vice-president-academic-vpa}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The VPA's tasks and responsibilities include:

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Developing an understanding of academic initiatives, integrated work experience, curriculum content, accreditation standards and requirements as well as any changes which could affect engineering education
   \item
    Determining the academic, moral, intellectual, cultural, social, and economic needs of Canadian engineering students, working towards changes if necessary, and offering national leverage in issues of negotiation between student representation and administration at member institutions and other relevant organizations
   \item
    Communicating relevant information and issues related to engineering education or that affect the Canadian Engineering student population to members and Engineering Deans Canada
   \item
    Facilitating the exchange of relevant academic information and advocacy efforts between members societies and national educational organizations
   \item
    Attending Canadian Engineering Accreditation Board and Engineering Deans Canada meetings while providing highlights of the meetings to the Board
   \item
    Managing policies and procedures that are of relevance to the Document of Stances, presenting appropriate positions on behalf of the National Executive, and conducting research regarding implications of potential stances, including both those put forth by the National Executive and members, and presenting these findings to the membership
   \item
    Directing the Advocacy Working Group, including organizing and chairing meetings, setting goals and directives, and monitoring the work of the Working Group
   \item
    Sending a questionnaire to members at the start of each mandate to survey their annual priorities and sharing the results with the membership to promote collaboration between engineering societies
   \item
    Creating and maintaining Letters of Support upon request from the Membership

  \end{enumerate}
 \item
  In support of the VPA, it is suggested that the following Commissioner position be available, to be managed by and support the VPA:
  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Data Commissioner
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Vice President Finance and Administration (VPFA)}
\label{vice-president-finance-and-administration-vpfa}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The VPFA's tasks and responsibilities include:

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Acting as the treasurer of CFES by:

    \begin{enumerate}
     \item
      Issuing outgoing payments
     \item
      Depositing incoming funds
     \item
      Tracking all financial assets
     \item
      Keeping complete and accurate record of all previous years financial records
     \item
      Creating and maintaining current and historical financial statements (income statement, balance sheet and cash flow statement)
    \end{enumerate}
   \item
    Ensuring the timely change of signing authorities on the CFES accounts at the end of the fiscal year.
   \item
    Cash membership fee cheque a maximum of one month after cheque has been received and a notice that the cheque has been cashed within two weeks after the cheque has been cashed.
   \item
    Preparing a list of membership fee collection information for the current and previous year
   \item
    Collecting the number of students represented by active members as specified in Bylaw 6.1 each year
   \item
    Submitting a proposed detailed budget for the incoming National Executive

    \begin{enumerate}
     \item
      Proposing amendments to this budget once the Board for the upcoming year is established.
    \end{enumerate}
   \item
    Documenting the support for expenses and revenues, including receipts, invoices, minutes approving expenditures, and any other necessary documents.
   \item
    Dealing with all incorporation matters, including filing the Annual Summary with Corporations Canada each year, using Corporation Number 0233129 and attaching any revision to the Constitution if applicable
   \item
    Ensuring that present trademark registrations are maintained
   \item
    If 75\% of the OTF is spent by the SDES of the budgeting year, the VPFA be required to reallocate funding from other parts of the budget and the change of the budget be voted at the SDES GA so the OTF can be sustained for the remainder of the budgeting year.
   \item
    Releasing to the Board the minutes of National Executive meetings within 30 days of a meeting taking place.
   \item
    Prior to the CESS, the VPFA must look at the member schools of each Board Member and the location of each conference and propose changes if necessary to ensure there is enough money in the respective funds.
   \item
    To ensure accountability and transparency, the VPFA shall complete the Financial Deliverables outlined in Section 8.10 of the Bylaws.
  \end{enumerate}
 \item
  In support of the VPFA, it is suggested that the following Commissioner positions be available, to be managed by and report to the VPFA:
  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Information Technology Commissioner
   \item
    Governance Commissioner
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Outgoing Vice-President Finance and Administration}
\label{outgoing-vice-president-finance-and-administration}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The Outgoing VPFA's tasks and responsibilities include:

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Work alongside the Vice-President Finance and Administration through the early months of their term
   \item
    Finalize the financial statements for the fiscal year of the outgoing National Executive.
   \item
    Ensure the completion of the audit for the fiscal year of the outgoing National Executive.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Vice President External (VPE)}
\label{vice-president-external-vpe}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The VPE's tasks and responsibilities include:

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Acting as the primary contact of the CFES to our national and international partners, and liaising with existing and prospective partners on behalf of the Federation
   \item
    Exploring new projects and cooperation ideas with existing and prospective national and international partners
   \item
    Ensuring that all partners and developing partners are invited to all relevant events
   \item
    Consulting with CFES member representatives when faced with major decisions
   \item
    Ensuring that all CFES members are aware of international opportunities and the benefits of international cooperation
   \item
    Providing regular updates on relevant projects, initiatives, and ongoing work of partners and the outcomes of these partnerships
   \item
    Attending meetings and other events as specified in the partnership agreements or finding a suitable representative, and providing a written report on the outcomes within 2 months
   \item
    Act as the primary contact to EngiQueers Canada and Engineers Canada, including coordinating their attendance at CFES events, as well as collaborative projects and advocacy
   \item
    Advocating to our external organizations in accordance with our Document of Stances in collaboration with the VPA
   \item
    Developing and maintaining contacts with other national or regional engineering educational organizations

  \end{enumerate}

 \item
  In support of the VPE, it is suggested that the following Commissioner positions be available, to be managed by and report to the VPE:
  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Corporate Relations Commissioner
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Vice President Services (VPS)}
\label{vice-president-services-vps}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The VPS's tasks and responsibilities include:

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Plan, schedule, and host monthly check-in meetings with the Activity Managers.
   \item
    Ensure that Activity Agreements are signed prior to acceptance of the bid.
   \item
    Respond to inquiries from Activity Managers in a timely manner and help direct their questions to appropriate members of the CFES team.
   \item
    Act as logistical and moral support at CFES activities, primarily to the Activity Managers.
   \item
    Ensuring that the responsibilities outlined in the Activity Agreements are completed with reference to the VPS best practices document and by deadlines stated within the agreement.
    \begin{enumerate}
     \item
      Including but not limited to the entirety of the Activity timeline and final deliverables; organizing committee transition reports, transition meeting with outgoing and incoming team and financial closeout.
    \end{enumerate}
   \item
    Train the Logistics Commissioner on the duties of the VPS and support them in their duties.
   \item
    Build on the accessibility training module continuously in collaboration with the leadership and development commissioner.
   \item
    Encouraging member societies to bid and host CFES Activities
    \begin{enumerate}
     \item
      Oversight and implementation of the Activity Bid Invitations procedure
    \end{enumerate}
   \item
    Work with the National Councillors to ensure the Incident Response Committee is implemented at each CFES Activity
   \item
    Distribute a Delegate Conference feedback form to each Activity Manager no later than ten days following the end of each CFES activity
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Meetings}
\label{meetings-1}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Meetings of the National Executive shall be held at any time and place to be determined by the members of such committee provided that two (2) days verbal notice of said meeting shall be given to each member of said committee.
 \item
  For there to be quorum, all four (4) members of the National Executive shall be present.
 \item
  Each member of the National Executive present at the meeting is authorized to exercise one (1) vote.
 \item
  If all National Executive members consent, one or more members may participate in a National Executive meeting by means of a technological communication method, such as the telephone, which allows all persons participating in the meeting to hear each other.
 \item
  National Executives participating in such a meeting by such means are deemed to be present at the meeting.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Dismissal}
\label{dismissal}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  A member of the National Executive may be dismissed by resolution of the Board adopted by two-thirds (2/3) of the Board present at such meeting convened to this end or by a resolution adopted by two-thirds (2/3) of the members of the General Assembly present at a meeting convened to this end.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Vacancy}
\label{vacancy-1}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Provided a vacancy occurs for any National Executive position, this vacancy shall be communicated to the membership within five (5) days.
 \item
  The Board may, by appointment, fill the vacancy using procedures consistent with this constitution, and a nomination period must be announced to the membership and remain open for a minimum of two (2) weeks.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Remuneration}
\label{remuneration-1}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The National Executive shall serve without remuneration and shall not, directly or indirectly, receive any profit from holding the position, provided that reasonable expenses incurred in the exercise of their duties are reimbursed.
\end{enumerate}
