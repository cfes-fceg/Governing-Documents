\section{Partner Organizations}\label{partner-organizations}

\subsection{Purpose}\label{purpose-1}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Partners are associated organizations who share in the vision and mission of the CFES.
 \item
  The Federation works with these groups to mutual benefit.
 \item
  New Partners must be recognized by resolution of the Board after the National Executive has ensured compliance with the provisions listed hereafter.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Student Partners}\label{student-partners}

\subsubsection{Purpose}\label{purpose-2}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The Federation shall have two types of Student Partners: Full Student Partners and Standing Student Partners.
 \item
  Contracts and/or partnership agreements with such organizations shall include the provisions, or provisions similar to those, contained in this section.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Full Student Partners}\label{full-student-partners}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The benefits Full Student Partners shall receive are as follows:
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Attend CELC with fee waived
 \item
  Attendance at other CFES events for a fee, unless otherwise decided through a ratified agreement
 \item
  Use of CFES network: May send emails to `member-link', logo on website under ``Student Partner'', advertisement of events and services

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    The requirements Full Student Partners must meet are as follows:
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Must have Governing Documents and be incorporated in the appropriate jurisdiction, whether country or province/state
 \item
  Must show purpose and direction, through Strategic Plan or other form of long term planning document
 \item
  Must show proof of having student members, such as emails
 \item
  Must have a logo
 \item
  Must have some sort of online presence, such as a website or Facebook page
 \item
  Must have a proposed document (i.e.~contract) with the CFES outlining relationship expectations at the time of the proposed partnership is brought to the Board
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Standing Student
 Partners}\label{standing-student-partners}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  The benefits Standing Student Partners shall receive are as follows:
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Visibility to CFES members
 \item
  Benefits as specified by the Board
 \item
  Not to be identified (on website or elsewhere) alongside Full Student Partners

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    The requirements Standing Student Partners must meet are as follows:
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Compatible with the Mission of the CFES
 \item
  Must be a student organization
 \item
  Must be assigned to International Relations Commissioner or Corporate Relations Commissioner, tracked and moved forward by the President
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Professional Partners}\label{professional-partners}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Professional Partners of the Federation are organizations who represent the interests of the engineering profession and with which the CFES has recognized agreements.
 \item
  While there are no specific requirements for this status beyond a resolution of the Board, the following provisions are recommended:
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Mission compatible with that of the CFES
 \item
  Services relevant to the membership
 \item
  Incorporated as a legal entity

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    A proposed Professional Partner must have a document (i.e. contract) with the CFES outlining relationship expectations at the time of the proposed partnership is brought to the Board.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Partnership Review}\label{partnership-review}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  An official partnership review report must be compiled every two years for CFES's current and developing student and professional partners.
 \item
  This will be lead by the VP-External and supported by the Board.
 \item
  The goal of this partnership review report is to ensure the CFES provide a transparent and thorough review of how each partner (or developing partner) aligns with the values of the Federation.
 \item
  Each partnership review must contain research and information about the following:
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}
 \item
  Organization Review

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Name
   \item
    Sector
   \item
    General Area of Work
   \item
    Mission and Vision
   \item
    Scope of Work
  \end{enumerate}
 \item
  Publicity and/or Concerns

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Links to Sources
  \end{enumerate}
 \item
  Values Comparison

  \begin{enumerate}
   \item
    Diversity
  \end{enumerate}
 \item
  Sustainability
 \item
  Quality student education
 \item
  Creation of safer spaces
 \item
  Respecting \& acknowledging Indigenity in engineering
 \item
  Dismantling oppressive systems
 \item
  Caring about people \& the environment
 \item
  Capacity to Engage and Influence
 \item
  Summary
 \item
  Additional comments by reviewer
\end{enumerate}
